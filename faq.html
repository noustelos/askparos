Paros Travel FAQ

Quick answers to the most common Paros travel questions. For anything else, try the chat on the homepage.

Which are the best beaches in Paros?

Kolymbithres is the most distinctive — granite rock formations and calm water inside Naousa bay. Santa Maria and Golden Beach (Chrissi Akti) are organized with water sports, Marcello and Krios are an easy swim across from Parikia, and Aliki and Faragas in the south feel gentler.

The wind matters: on meltemi days the west and south coasts are the calmer choice.

Where is the best sunset spot in Paros?

Parikia's west-facing waterfront and the windmill by the port are the classics. The Paros Park amphitheater area near Monastiri is a quieter option, and the Pounta side looks across to Antiparos as the sun sets behind it.

Is Antiparos worth a day trip, and how do I get there?

Yes — it's one of the easiest Cycladic day trips. The car ferry from Pounta crosses in about ten minutes, and in season passenger boats also run from Parikia. Visit the cave with its stalactites (paid entry), wander Antiparos town and finish with a swim near the port. Check current schedules, as they change by season.

What can I do in Paros when it's windy?

Paros gets the meltemi wind in high summer. Head to the sheltered west and south coasts (Parikia bay, Aliki, Faragas), go inland to Lefkes and the Byzantine path to Prodromos, or watch the windsurfers at Golden Beach and New Golden Beach — the island turned the wind into a sport.

How do I get around Paros?

Ferries arrive at Parikia, and the airport is in the south near Aliki. Buses connect Parikia with Naousa, Lefkes, Piso Livadi and the main beaches — check the current schedule, as times change by season. Taxis are limited in peak season, so book transfers ahead in July and August.

Do I need a car in Paros?

It depends on where you stay and your plans. Buses cover Parikia, Naousa, Lefkes and the main beaches well; a car or ATV helps for remote corners like Faragas or the ancient marble quarries at Marathi. Parking in Naousa is difficult in August, so book rentals early in season.

Which is the best beach for families in Paros?

Santa Maria and Golden Beach are organized with shallow entries; Aliki is a calm fishing village with tavernas right by the sand; Livadia sits next to Parikia. Distances are short, which keeps family days simple.

Where can I windsurf in Paros?

Golden Beach and New Golden Beach on the southeast coast are among Europe's best-known windsurfing spots, with schools and rental centers for every level. Santa Maria also runs water sports on lighter days. High season books up, so reserve lessons ahead.
