# Food & Drink — Paros

Purpose:
Give visitors authentic local food direction without naming venues.

## Local Specialties

- Gouna: sun-dried mackerel grilled over coals — a Naousa harbor classic.
- Fresh fish and seafood: the fishing harbors (Naousa, Aliki, Piso Livadi) are the
  natural places to look.
- Local cheeses: graviera-style and soft local cheeses appear on most menus.
- Chickpeas, capers, local honey and island vegetables feature in traditional dishes.
- Paros wine: the island has an ancient wine tradition and active local wineries;
  look for local white (Monemvasia grape) and rosé/red blends (Mandilaria).
- Souma: the local grape spirit, often offered after a meal.

## Where (Areas, Not Venues)

- Naousa: waterfront tables around the little harbor; busy, atmospheric, pricier.
- Parikia: old-town lanes and the sunset-facing waterfront.
- Lefkes, Marpissa, Prodromos: village squares, more local pace and prices.
- Aliki & Piso Livadi: fish tavernas by the water, calmer mood.

## How To Answer

- Match area to the visitor's base and mood; note that view usually raises price.
- Recommend dishes and areas, never specific restaurants.
- Wine tastings at local wineries are paid experiences — confirm visits ahead.

## Do Not Invent

- No restaurant, bar or winery names.
- No prices, no "best restaurant" claims, no opening hours.
