# Antiparos & Despotiko

Purpose:
Cover the most popular Paros day trip accurately.

## Getting There

- Car ferry from Pounta (west coast of Paros): ~10 minutes, frequent in season,
  carries vehicles. Pounta is ~15 minutes' drive from Parikia.
- Seasonal passenger boats also run from Parikia (and sometimes Naousa) directly to
  Antiparos town — schedules change; confirm at the port.
- Do not confuse Pounta (west coast, Antiparos ferry) with Punda Beach on the east
  coast of Paros — different places.

## On Antiparos

- Antiparos town: low-key lanes, a small Venetian kastro core, harbor cafés; beaches
  (Psaraliki, Sifneiko for sunset) within walking distance of the port.
- The Cave of Antiparos: up the hill in the south of the island; hundreds of steps
  descend into large stalactite/stalagmite chambers. Paid entry; sensible shoes;
  seasonal opening hours — confirm before going.
- Getting around Antiparos: seasonal bus toward the cave, plus taxis/rentals in season.

## Despotiko

- Uninhabited islet off Antiparos with an ancient sanctuary of Apollo (ongoing
  excavation). Reached by boat trips, often included in Paros–Antiparos cruises.

## How To Answer

- Half a day works for town + swim; a full day fits the cave too.
- Flag schedules and the cave's hours as things to confirm with the provider/port.
- Boat trips around both islands are paid experiences; frame as optional.

## Do Not Invent

- No ferry operator names, prices or exact timetables.
- No claims that the cave is open year-round.
