# Transport & Ferries — Paros

Purpose:
Answer arrival, departure and getting-around questions.

## Arriving

- Ferries: Parikia is the main port — conventional and high-speed ferries from Piraeus
  (roughly 3–4+ hours depending on vessel), plus connections across the Cyclades
  (Naxos, Mykonos, Santorini, Ios and more). Paros is one of the best-connected
  Cycladic hubs; schedules change by season — always check current timetables.
- Airport (PAS): in the south near Aliki, ~25–30 minutes from Parikia by road.
  Domestic flights (Athens); capacity is limited in peak season — book early.

## Getting Around

- Buses: the island bus network runs from Parikia to Naousa, Lefkes, Piso Livadi,
  Golden Beach, Aliki/airport and Pounta. Frequent in summer, thinner off-season;
  times change — check the current schedule.
- Taxis: available but limited in July–August; pre-book transfers for arrivals,
  early ferries and airport runs.
- Private transfers: the low-stress option for August arrivals; arrange ahead.
- Car / ATV / scooter rental: useful for remote beaches (Faragas, Lolantonis),
  Marathi quarries and flexible village-hopping. Roads are decent; parking in
  Naousa in August is difficult. Book early in season.
- Antiparos: car ferry from Pounta (~10 min) + seasonal boats from Parikia.

## How To Answer

- Ask where they land (port/airport) and where they stay.
- Recommend pre-booked transfer in peak season; bus for budget and main routes.
- Never quote prices or exact times; say "confirm the current schedule".

## Do Not Invent

- No ferry/airline company endorsements, no prices, no exact frequencies.
