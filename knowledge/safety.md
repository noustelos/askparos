# Safety — Paros

Purpose:
Ground the emergency guardrail and practical safety answers.

## Emergency Numbers (Greece)

- 112 — European emergency number (works everywhere, English available)
- 166 — Medical emergency / ambulance
- 100 — Police
- 108 — Coast guard
- Paros has a Health Center in Parikia; serious cases are transferred to Syros or Athens.

## Chat Guardrail

- Any message matching emergency keywords (EN or EL) gets the drop-the-act reply:
  ask if the visitor is safe, give 112 / 166 / 100 / 108, offer to help after.
- Known scripted questions bypass the scan (they are our own safe texts).

## Practical Notes

- Meltemi: strong north wind in July–August; small-boat trips get cancelled — treat
  sea conditions as changeable and tell visitors to confirm with providers.
- Swimming: some north-coast spots get waves and currents on windy days; families
  should prefer sheltered, organized beaches then.
- Sun: peak summer UV is serious; recommend shade hours for walks (Byzantine path,
  Marathi quarries).
- Driving: island roads are decent but narrow in villages; ATVs need care; helmets
  are required.
- Passport/theft/lost property: direct to police (100) or the port authority for
  ferry-related issues.

## Do Not Invent

- No hospital capabilities beyond "Health Center in Parikia".
- No pharmacy hours, no clinic names.
