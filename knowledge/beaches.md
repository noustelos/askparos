# Beaches in Paros

Purpose:
Help visitors choose beaches based on mood, transport, family needs and wind.

Core bot rule:
Answer first. Recommend second. Sell third.

## Quick Guidance

- Kolymbithres: the signature beach — sculpted granite rock formations forming small
  coves inside Naousa bay; calm water, iconic scenery.
- Monastiri: next to Kolymbithres, by Paros Park; sheltered, organized, family-friendly.
- Santa Maria: northeast of Naousa; organized, water sports, shallow entry.
- Golden Beach (Chrissi Akti): long sandy beach on the southeast; windsurfing hub.
- New Golden Beach (Nea Chrissi Akti): next bay north; core windsurfing spot in meltemi.
- Logaras / Piso Livadi: east coast, easy and organized, near the small harbor.
- Marcello & Krios: across Parikia bay; easy swim near town, sunset views back at Parikia.
- Livadia: right next to Parikia; practical, family-easy.
- Aliki: southern fishing village; gentle, shallow, tavernas by the sand.
- Faragas: south; sheltered on meltemi days, quieter mood.
- Lolantonis / Glyfa: southeast, quieter, easier with a car.

## Wind-Safe Thinking

- The meltemi (north wind) blows mainly July–August.
- Exposed on strong north wind: Santa Maria, Kolymbithres can chop up; the southeast
  coast gets the waves windsurfers want.
- Sheltered picks on windy days: west and south — Parikia bay side, Aliki, Faragas.
- If sea conditions are poor, suggest Parikia lanes, Lefkes, food or villages instead
  of forcing a beach plan.

## How To Answer Beach Questions

- Ask where the visitor is staying if they ask "near me".
- Mention transport limits, parking (Naousa in August) and taxi scarcity when relevant.
- Mention wind and sea conditions without claiming live weather.
- Give 2–4 options, not a huge list.
- Match the recommendation to mood: easy, family, scenic, water sports, quiet.

## Good Answer Pattern

1. Best practical option
2. Why it fits
3. Alternative
4. Transport or wind note

## Do Not Invent

- No beach bar or club names.
- No prices for sunbeds or rentals.
- No claims about lifeguards or facilities beyond "organized/unorganized".
