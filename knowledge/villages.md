# Villages of Paros

Purpose:
Guide visitors around the island's towns and inland villages.

## The Two Towns

- Parikia: capital and main ferry port. Old town around the Frankish Kastro (built with
  ancient marble), whitewashed lanes, west-facing waterfront with the windmill by the
  port. Home of Panagia Ekatontapiliani ("Church of a Hundred Doors") — a 4th-century
  Byzantine complex, one of the most important early-Christian monuments in Greece.
- Naousa: fishing harbor town in the north. Small Venetian kastro ruin on the harbor
  islet, caique boats, waterfront tavernas, the island's most photographed evening spot.
  Busy in August; parking is hard.

## Inland & Smaller Villages

- Lefkes: old mountain capital, amphitheatrically built; marble lanes, views toward
  Naxos. Start of the Byzantine path (a paved old trail) to Prodromos — a popular walk
  (~1 hour, downhill direction easier).
- Prodromos: whitewashed farming village with an arched entrance; quiet, lived-in.
- Marpissa: traditional village near the east coast, windmills, narrow lanes.
- Piso Livadi: small fishing harbor on the east coast, beaches nearby.
- Aliki: southern fishing village, gentle beach, near the airport.
- Drios: quiet southeast coastal village.
- Marathi: site of the ancient Parian marble quarries — the marble of the Venus de Milo
  and the Hermes of Praxiteles. Unfenced ruins, atmospheric; wear proper shoes.

## How To Answer

- Naousa vs Parikia: Naousa = postcard harbor evenings; Parikia = history, port
  convenience, sunset waterfront. Neither is "wrong"; ask about mood and base.
- Quiet-Paros requests → Lefkes, Marpissa, Prodromos, Aliki; suggest late afternoon.
- Mention the Byzantine path for walkers; note summer heat at midday.

## Do Not Invent

- No shop, taverna or hotel names.
- No opening hours for churches or sites — say "confirm locally".
